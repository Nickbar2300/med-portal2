Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 7, 2026

Keystone Health Partners ("we," "us," or "our") operates the Rapid Records medical records retrieval platform. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our services.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information necessary to provide medical records retrieval services, including:

  • Patient Information: Name, date of birth, phone number, email address, and Medicare Beneficiary Identifier (MBI) provided with patient authorization
  • Medicare Claims Data: Health claims information accessed through the CMS Blue Button 2.0 API with explicit patient authorization via OAuth 2.0
  • HIPAA Authorization: Signed authorization forms including patient signatures, timestamps, and consent records
  • Identity Verification: Identity verification data processed through CLEAR identity verification services
  • Usage Data: Log data, IP addresses, and browser information related to portal access

2. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information exclusively to:

  • Retrieve and deliver authorized Medicare claims records to designated law firms
  • Verify patient identity prior to records access
  • Generate AI-assisted medical record summaries for authorized legal purposes
  • Comply with HIPAA requirements and applicable law
  • Maintain audit logs as required by federal and state law

3. CMS Blue Button 2.0 API

We access Medicare claims data through the CMS Blue Button 2.0 API only after receiving explicit patient authorization through Medicare.gov. Patients may revoke access at any time through their Medicare.gov account. We access only the minimum necessary data required to fulfill the authorized records request.

4. HIPAA Compliance

We operate as a Business Associate under HIPAA. All Protected Health Information (PHI) is:

  • Encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher
  • Encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption
  • Retained only for the minimum period necessary (signed HIPAA forms: 90 days; raw claims data: session only)
  • Accessible only to authorized personnel with a legitimate need
  • Subject to Business Associate Agreements with all service providers handling PHI

5. Data Retention

  • Signed HIPAA authorization forms: 90 days, then automatically deleted
  • Raw Medicare claims data: Not stored server-side; exists only in the browser session
  • AI-generated reports: Delivered to authorized law firm; not retained server-side
  • Request metadata: Retained for duration of active case plus one year

6. Information Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or rent patient information. We share information only:

  • With the authorized law firm that initiated the records request
  • With service providers operating under HIPAA Business Associate Agreements (Microsoft Azure, Twilio, CLEAR)
  • As required by law or valid legal process
  • With patient consent for purposes beyond those stated here

7. Patient Rights

Patients have the right to:

  • Revoke Medicare data access at any time via Medicare.gov
  • Request deletion of their information from our systems
  • Receive a copy of their information we hold
  • File a complaint with the HHS Office for Civil Rights

8. Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your information, including role-based access controls, audit logging, multi-factor authentication, and regular security assessments.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We will notify affected parties of material changes. Continued use of our services after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
